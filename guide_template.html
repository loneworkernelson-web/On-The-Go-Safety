%%ORG_NAME%% — Worker Safety App Guide

Your complete guide to the OTG Lone Worker Safety App. Keep this document handy during your first week of use.

What this app does: The OTG Safety App lets you log where you are, track how long you've been there, and instantly alert your contacts and your organisation's safety monitor if something goes wrong. It works offline and runs as a standalone app on your phone's home screen.
⚠️ The app does NOT call emergency services. In a life-threatening emergency, always call 111 (or your local emergency number) first. The SOS button is a notification tool — it alerts your contacts and HQ monitor, but is not a substitute for calling emergency services.

1. Installation — Add to Your Home Screen

The safety app is a Progressive Web App (PWA). You must install it onto your phone's home screen. Running it only in a browser window will prevent offline use, GPS background tracking, and audio alerts from working correctly.

🤖 Android

  1. Open the link your manager sent in Chrome.
  2. Tap the Menu (⋮) button.
  3. Tap "Install App" or "Add to Home Screen".
  4. Tap Install to confirm.

🍎 iPhone / iPad

  1. Open the link in Safari (not Chrome).
  2. Tap the Share button (□ with arrow).
  3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen".
  4. Tap Add in the top right.
💡 iPhone tip: Always launch the app from the home screen icon — not from your browser history. The two behave differently; only the home screen version will work correctly offline and will be able to sound audio alerts.

2. First-Run Setup Wizard

The first time you open the app from your home screen, a setup wizard will guide you through three steps. This only happens once.

  1. Your Identity: Enter your full name exactly as it appears in your organisation's records — First Last. This is case-sensitive and must match the Staff list precisely. Also enter your phone number and email address.
  2. Safety & Duress PINs: Create a 4-digit Safety PIN (e.g. 1234) — you'll use this to confirm you're safe after an alarm. Then create a different secret Duress PIN (e.g. 9999). If someone forces you to cancel an alarm, enter this instead — it appears to cancel normally but silently sends a DURESS alert with your GPS to your contacts and HQ.
  3. Response Hierarchy: Enter your Primary Contact (e.g. spouse or next-of-kin) and, optionally, an Escalation Contact (e.g. your manager). Both contacts can have a name, phone number, email, and ntfy topic for push alerts.

⚡ Critical Timing Mode

Below the duration buttons you will see a Critical Timing toggle. When switched on, the grace period is removed — if you have not ended your visit by the due time, the alarm fires immediately rather than waiting for the usual grace period your organisation has configured.

Use Critical Timing whenever your situation calls for a tighter safety net — for example, a home visit to an unfamiliar person, a late-night appointment, or any time you feel a little less certain about your safety than usual. You do not need a specific reason; it is there for any time you would like the system to be more responsive.

  • Auto-enabled: Sites your organisation has marked as high-risk will have Critical Timing turned on automatically when you select them. You can still toggle it off if you choose.
  • Manual: You can switch it on yourself for any visit, regardless of the site's risk rating.
  • Per-visit setting: Critical Timing resets after each visit — it does not carry over automatically unless the site is flagged as high-risk.
💡 When in doubt, turn it on. The only consequence of Critical Timing is a more prompt alarm — there is no penalty for using it. If your visit simply takes a little longer than expected, tap Extend on the locked screen.

6. During a Visit

Once a visit is active, the app moves to a locked screen — this is intentional. It conserves your battery and keeps the GPS running in the background.

Mid-Visit Controls

The locked screen has four controls always available during an active visit:

  • SOS button — the large red circular button in the top-right corner. Tap it 3 times rapidly (within about 2 seconds) to raise an alarm. See Section 14: In an Emergency.
  • Extend — tap the blue Extend button to give yourself more time before the overtime alert fires. Choose a new duration from the options presented.
  • 📋 Forms — tap the Forms button to open your organisation's forms library. Use this to submit a status update, a note, or any other mid-visit documentation without ending the visit.
  • End Visit pill — slide the red End Visit pill from left to right to begin the departure process.

Battery Saver

After 10 seconds without interaction, the app automatically switches to Battery Saver mode. The screen goes black and the system navigation bars are hidden — this is normal behaviour. GPS tracking and all safety monitoring continue to run in the background.

8. Travel Mode

Use Travel Mode — started via the Trip button in the action row — whenever you are in transit between locations. It monitors your route under safety monitoring and, if your organisation uses travel reports, calculates the distance travelled using OpenStreetMap as the reference.

Starting a trip

  1. Tap Trip in the action row (the three buttons above the location list). The Trip button activates and a General button appears alongside it. The location tiles remain fully visible so you can choose your destination.
  2. Choose your destination:
    • Travelling to a specific site — tap its tile in the location list. The tile highlights and a confirmation banner appears above the footer showing the destination name.
    • General travel — tap the General button if you're not heading to a listed site (e.g. a one-off address or personal errand). A banner confirms "General travel" is selected.
  3. Once a destination is confirmed, the footer expands to show your options — set the expected duration of your trip, and optionally tick "No report required" if this is personal travel (see below).
  4. Slide the Begin pill to start the trip. If your organisation requires a pre-departure form, it will appear at this point — complete and submit it to proceed.
💡 General vs named destination: If you tap a named tile after selecting General, General deselects and the tile becomes your destination. You can switch freely before sliding to begin.

GPS tracking during travel

While a trip is active, the app sends your GPS location to the safety monitor at regular intervals. Keep the app open on the in-app locked screen for the duration of your journey to ensure continuous tracking.

⚠️ Do not use your phone's native lock button during a trip. Because this app is a Progressive Web App (PWA) running in your browser, activating your phone's own lock screen is likely to suspend GPS and background updates. This is a known limitation of PWAs on mobile devices. Instead, leave the app running on its own locked screen — this is what it is designed for. If your phone dims or goes dark on its own, that is fine; just do not press the side button to lock it manually.

Personal travel — no report required

If you're travelling for personal reasons and no report is needed, tick the "No report required" checkbox in the expanded footer before you begin. The trip will still be safety-monitored, but you will not be asked to submit a report when you finish. If your organisation mandates reports for all travel, this checkbox will not be available.

Pre-Departure Form

Your organisation may require you to complete a short questionnaire before a travel visit begins — covering vehicle condition, route details, or a fit-to-drive confirmation. If this is configured, the form will appear automatically after you slide to begin and before your trip timer starts.

You must submit the form to proceed. The answers are sent to your organisation as part of the visit record. If you close the form without completing it, the trip will not start.

Travel Report — pre-filled fields

When you end a travel trip and the Travel Report template loads, several fields at the top of the form are filled in automatically:

  • Start Point — the address where your trip began, resolved from your GPS location at trip start. The field may briefly show "Locating…" while the address is being looked up. If GPS or network was unavailable, raw coordinates are shown instead.
  • Destination — the name and address of your selected site, or "General travel" if you chose no specific destination.
  • Estimated distance — a distance figure calculated from your GPS track during the journey.
Always check the pre-filled fields before submitting. The start point, destination, and distance are calculated automatically and are usually accurate — but GPS drift, poor signal, or a diverted route can affect them. Correct anything that looks wrong before you slide to submit.

Offline Mode

The app works without an internet connection. All safety events — visit start, GPS pulses, alarms, departure reports — are queued locally on your device and uploaded automatically as soon as connectivity is restored. A blue 📡 OFFLINE MODE — Data queued for upload banner appears at the top of the screen whenever you are offline.

  • You will not lose any safety data due to poor signal.
  • If the queue reaches 50 unsent items, the app will warn you — this almost always indicates a persistent connectivity problem rather than a brief outage.
  • To manually trigger an upload when you know you're back online, go to ⚙️ Settings → Diagnostics → Flush Outbox Now.
💡 First load requires internet. The app must be opened at least once with a connection so it can cache the configuration. After that, it loads and runs fully offline.

Changing Phones

The app ties your registration to a specific device. If you get a new phone, change browsers, or need to reinstall, there are a few things to be aware of:

  • Export your personal sites first. Any locations you have added manually (via + Add site) are stored locally on your device. Go to ⚙️ Settings → Export My Sites before switching devices. You will receive a short export code — paste it into Import Sites on your new device to restore them.
  • Your admin must clear your device registration. The app locks each worker name to the device it was first registered on. If you try to register on a new device, you will see "This worker is already registered on another device." Your administrator needs to clear your device registration in the Staff sheet before you can re-register. Contact them before switching devices if possible.
  • Do not clear your browser cache without a backup. Clearing your browser's cache or site data deletes all locally stored app settings — your name, PINs, contacts, and personal sites. This also generates a new device ID, which will again block re-registration until your admin clears it. If you must clear your cache, export your personal sites first and contact your admin.

13. Instant Push Notifications (ntfy)

Why this exists

When an alarm fires, your contacts are notified by email. Email is reliable, but it has a problem: most people don't have email open on their phone during the day, and notifications can be delayed by minutes or silenced entirely.

ntfy solves this. ntfy is a free, open-source push notification service. When it is set up, your contacts will receive an instant alert on their phone the moment any alarm fires — directly on their lock screen, with no email delay. Think of it as a direct line to your contacts' phones, separate from email.

ntfy is optional but strongly recommended. Your safety system works without it — email and SMS will still be sent. But for genuine emergencies, the seconds saved by a push notification versus an email can matter. The setup steps below are important — skipping them means notifications may be delayed or silent.

How it works

ntfy uses topics — private channel names that only you and your contact know. Your contact subscribes to their own topic in the ntfy app. You enter that topic name into your safety app. When an alarm fires, the system posts a notification directly to that topic, and your contact's phone wakes up instantly.

Topics are private by design — they are just text strings, and nobody can find or subscribe to yours unless you share the name with them.

Step 1 — Your contact installs ntfy and subscribes

  1. Download the free ntfy app — available from the iOS App Store, Google Play, or by visiting ntfy.sh in a browser.
  2. Open the app and tap "Subscribe to topic".
  3. Choose a private topic name — something memorable but not easily guessed. For example: jane-safety-7x9k2 or smithj-alerts-2026. Avoid anything obvious like a full name alone.
  4. Tap Subscribe. The app will now listen for notifications on that topic.
  5. Share the topic name with you — you'll need to enter it in your safety app.
💡 One topic per contact: Each contact needs their own unique topic. Your Emergency Contact and Escalation Contact should each choose a different name and share it with you separately.

Step 2 — Critical phone settings (do not skip)

By default, Android and iOS aggressively restrict background apps to save battery. Without the settings below, ntfy notifications may arrive late — or not at all — when the phone screen is off. Each contact must complete the steps for their phone type.

Android

  1. Enable Instant Delivery — open the ntfy app, go to Settings, and turn on Instant Delivery. This keeps a persistent background connection to the server so notifications arrive immediately even when the phone is in sleep mode. Without this, Android's doze mode can delay messages by many minutes. A small permanent notification will appear in the status bar confirming the service is running — this is normal and expected.
  2. Disable battery optimisation for ntfy — go to phone Settings → Apps → ntfy → Battery and select Unrestricted (the exact wording varies by phone brand). This prevents Android from killing the ntfy background service. On Samsung: Settings → Apps → ntfy → Battery → Unrestricted. On Xiaomi/MIUI: Settings → Apps → Manage Apps → ntfy → Battery Saver → No restrictions. The ntfy app itself will show a warning banner if this has not been set — tap it to go directly to the correct screen.
  3. Set the notification channel to override Do Not Disturb — in the ntfy app, long-press the subscription you just created and tap Edit. Under Notifications, tap the channel name, then tap Override Do Not Disturb and enable it. This ensures alarms break through DND and silent profiles.
  4. Set notification importance to Urgent — in the same notification channel settings (above), set Importance to Urgent. This makes the notification sound immediately and appear as a pop-up on the lock screen.

iPhone (iOS)

  1. Allow notifications — when ntfy first opens, accept the prompt to allow notifications. If you declined it previously, go to phone Settings → ntfy → Notifications and turn on Allow Notifications. Set Alert style to Banners and enable Show on Lock Screen and Show in Notification Centre.
  2. Enable Time-Sensitive Notifications — in Settings → ntfy → Notifications, turn on Time Sensitive Notifications. This allows ntfy alerts to break through Focus modes and Do Not Disturb.
  3. Allow ntfy through your Focus modes — if you use Focus or Do Not Disturb, go to Settings → Focus → [your Focus mode] → Apps and add ntfy to the allowed apps list, or ensure Time Sensitive is enabled for that Focus. Otherwise ntfy notifications will be silenced during Focus.
  4. Check notification sound is set — in Settings → ntfy → Notifications, ensure a sound is selected (not "None"). Without a sound, the notification will appear silently on the lock screen.
⚠ iPhone mute switch limitation: The ntfy app does not currently support iOS Critical Alerts — the notification tier that overrides the physical mute switch. If an iPhone contact has their mute switch on (the small toggle on the side of the phone), ntfy notifications will arrive silently. Contacts should be aware of this and consider leaving their mute switch off, or keeping their phone on a surface where a screen flash is visible. This is a known limitation of the ntfy iOS app and is not something that can be configured around.

14. In an Emergency

🚨 IMPORTANT — Read Before You Need It

The SOS button does NOT call emergency services. In a life-threatening emergency, call 111 (NZ), 000 (Australia), 999 (UK), or 911 (USA/Canada) first. The SOS function is a notification tool for your contacts and your organisation's safety monitor — it does not automatically dispatch police or ambulance.

Alarm outside an active visit — Decoy Lock Screen

If you trigger an alarm via shake when you do not have an active visit running (for example, before you have started a visit, or after you have departed), the app cannot show the normal alarm overlay — there is no locked visit screen to overlay. Instead, it displays a decoy lock screen.

The decoy screen is designed to be indistinguishable from a real phone lock screen to a casual bystander — it shows the current time and date, an "Enter Passcode" prompt, and a standard numeric keypad. The alarm is already active and your contacts are being notified in the background.

  • To confirm you are safe: enter your Safety PIN on the decoy keypad. The screen dismisses and the alarm is cancelled.
  • Under duress: enter your Duress PIN instead. The screen appears to dismiss normally, but a silent DURESS alert is sent to your contacts and the safety monitor.
  • An incorrect PIN causes the keypad to shake — try again.
The decoy screen is not your phone's real lock screen. It cannot make emergency calls, and swiping or pressing the home button will not bypass it during an active alarm.

  • A what3words address (e.g. ///filled.count.soap) in the emergency email, if that feature is configured
