%%ORG_NAME%% — Worker Safety App Guide

Your complete guide to the OTG Lone Worker Safety App. Keep this document handy during your first week of use.

What this app does: The OTG Safety App lets you log where you are, track how long you've been there, and instantly alert your contacts and your organisation's safety monitor if something goes wrong. It works offline and runs as a standalone app on your phone's home screen.
⚠️ The app does NOT call emergency services. In a life-threatening emergency, always call 111 (or your local emergency number) first. The SOS button is a notification tool — it alerts your contacts and HQ monitor, but is not a substitute for calling emergency services.

1. Installation — Add to Your Home Screen

The safety app is a Progressive Web App (PWA). You must install it onto your phone's home screen. Running it only in a browser window will prevent offline use, GPS background tracking, and audio alerts from working correctly.

🤖 Android

  1. Open the link your manager sent in Chrome.
  2. Tap the Menu (⋮) button.
  3. Tap "Install App" or "Add to Home Screen".
  4. Tap Install to confirm.

🍎 iPhone / iPad

  1. Open the link in Safari (not Chrome).
  2. Tap the Share button (□ with arrow).
  3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen".
  4. Tap Add in the top right.
💡 iPhone tip: Always launch the app from the home screen icon — not from your browser history. The two behave differently; only the home screen version will work correctly offline and will be able to sound audio alerts.

2. First-Run Setup Wizard

The first time you open the app from your home screen, a setup wizard will guide you through four steps. This only happens once.

  1. Your Name: Enter your full name exactly as it appears in your organisation's records — First Last. This is case-sensitive and must match the Staff list precisely.
  2. Safety PIN: Create a 4-digit PIN (e.g. 1234). You'll use this to confirm you're safe after an alarm.
  3. Duress PIN: Create a different secret PIN (e.g. 9999). If someone forces you to cancel an alarm, enter this PIN instead. It appears to cancel the alarm but silently sends a DURESS alert with your GPS to your contacts and HQ.
  4. Emergency Contact: Your personal emergency contact (e.g. spouse or next-of-kin).
  5. Escalation Contact: Your work escalation contact (e.g. your manager).

Syncing the Location List

Tap the Sync button to download the latest approved locations from your organisation. This requires an internet connection. Once synced, locations are available offline.

Adding a Manual Location

If you're visiting somewhere not on the list (e.g. a new client):

  1. Tap "+ Add Manual Location" at the bottom of the list.
  2. Enter the Business Name and Site Name clearly.
  3. For the address: if you're already on-site, tap the 📍 GPS button to fill it in automatically. Otherwise, type it manually.
  4. Set whether a Report is Required on departure (if applicable).
  5. Tap Save.

Editing a Location

Tap the small ℹ button on any tile you created, or on the Travelling tile, to edit its settings. You cannot edit organisation-provided locations.

Site Safety Information

Tap the ℹ button on any location tile to open the site info panel. If your organisation has uploaded emergency procedures or hazard documentation for that site, a red 🛡️ VIEW SITE SAFETY INFORMATION button will appear.

Tapping it opens a self-contained safety page in your browser — no Google account required. The page includes any photos or documents your organisation has attached to that site.

If no procedures have been uploaded yet, the button is replaced with a prompt to submit documentation. Tap it to open the procedures form for that site.

6. Ending a Visit

  1. Open the app — the locked screen shows the "End Visit" pill at the bottom. Slide it from left to right to begin the departure process.
  2. If a report form is required for this location, fill it in — notes, photos, signature as prompted.
  3. Slide the "Submit & End" pill from left to right to send your report and close the visit.
  4. A green toast notification (✅ Report Sent) will appear at the top of the screen. No "OK" button needed — this is intentional.
  5. The app will announce "Visit concluded." (or "Travel concluded." for a travel trip) aloud via your phone's text-to-speech. Make sure your phone volume is not muted during visits.

7. Travel Mode

Use the Travelling tile for time spent in transit between locations.

Personal Travel

If you're travelling for personal reasons (not work-related), tap the ℹ button on the Travelling tile and set "Report Required" to No. The app will not ask for mileage or a report when you finish.

Business Travel

For work travel, ensure "Report Required" is set to Yes. When you end the trip, the app will calculate your distance and — if mileage tracking is enabled for your organisation — ask for your odometer reading.

💡 Travel settings persist: Your preferred setting for the Travelling tile is remembered between sessions unless your organisation mandates reports for all travel, in which case it cannot be changed.

11. Instant Push Notifications (ntfy)

Why this exists

When an alarm fires, your contacts are notified by email. Email is reliable, but it has a problem: most people don't have email open on their phone during the day, and notifications can be delayed by minutes or silenced entirely.

ntfy solves this. ntfy is a free, open-source push notification service. When it is set up, your contacts will receive an instant alert on their phone the moment any alarm fires — directly on their lock screen, with no email delay. It works even when the phone is locked and silent. Think of it as a direct line to your contacts' phones, separate from email.

ntfy is optional but strongly recommended. Your safety system works without it — email and SMS will still be sent. But for genuine emergencies, the seconds saved by a push notification versus an email can matter.

How it works

ntfy uses topics — private channel names that only you and your contact know. Your contact subscribes to their own topic in the ntfy app. You enter that topic name into your safety app. When an alarm fires, the system posts a notification directly to that topic, and your contact's phone wakes up instantly.

Topics are private by design — they are just text strings, and nobody can find or subscribe to yours unless you share the name with them.

What your contact needs to do (they do this once)

  1. Download the free ntfy app on their phone — available from the iOS App Store, Google Play, or by visiting ntfy.sh in a browser.
  2. Open the app and tap "Subscribe to topic".
  3. Choose a private topic name — something memorable but not easily guessed. For example: jane-safety-7x9k2 or smithj-alerts-2026. Avoid anything obvious like your full name alone.
  4. Tap Subscribe. The app will now listen for notifications on that topic.
  5. Tell you the topic name — you'll need to enter it in your safety app.
💡 One topic per contact: Each contact needs their own topic. Your Emergency Contact and Escalation Contact should each choose a different topic name and give it to you separately.

What you need to do as the worker

Once your contact has shared their topic name with you, enter it in the safety app. There are two ways to do this:

During the Setup Wizard (first-time setup): On the Emergency Contact and Escalation Contact screens, there is an optional field labelled "ntfy topic". Type in the topic name your contact gave you.

After setup, via Settings: Go to the app's Settings screen and find the Emergency Contact or Escalation Contact section. There will be an "ntfy topic" field — enter or update the topic name there.

💡 Verify it works: After entering the topic name, send a Test Alert from the Settings screen. Your contact should receive a push notification within a few seconds. The test email they receive will also include a one-tap subscribe link for convenience. If they don't receive the push, check the topic name was entered correctly — it must match exactly, including capitalisation.

What your contacts will receive

When any alarm fires — SOS, DURESS, overdue, or welfare check failure — your contacts will receive a push notification on their phone showing:

  • The alarm type (e.g. 🚨 PANIC / SOS or ⚠ OVERDUE)
  • Your name and phone number
  • The site you were at and its address
  • A GPS link (if location was available)

When you mark yourself safe (the alarm is resolved), your contacts also receive an ✅ All Clear push notification so they know the situation has been resolved.

ntfy is free and open source. Your organisation may run its own private ntfy server for additional privacy — if so, your administrator will have configured this and nothing changes from your perspective. If no custom server is configured, the free public service at ntfy.sh is used.

12. In an Emergency

🚨 IMPORTANT — Read Before You Need It

The SOS button does NOT call emergency services. In a life-threatening emergency, call 111 (NZ), 000 (Australia), 999 (UK), or 911 (USA/Canada) first. The SOS function is a notification tool for your contacts and your organisation's safety monitor — it does not automatically dispatch police or ambulance.

Raising an Alarm (SOS)

  1. Tap the large red SOS button 3 times rapidly.
  2. The screen turns red and begins broadcasting your GPS location.
  3. Your emergency contact, escalation contact, and your organisation's monitor are alerted immediately.
  4. Continue to move to safety and call emergency services if needed.

Cancelling an Alarm (Normal)

  1. When you are safe, hold the green "I Am Safe" button.
  2. Enter your standard 4-digit PIN.
  3. The alarm is cancelled and an "All Clear" message is sent to your contacts and the monitor.

Cancelling Under Duress

If someone is watching you and forcing you to "cancel" an alarm:

  1. Hold the "I Am Safe" button as normal.
  2. Enter your Duress PIN (the different one you set up during setup).
  3. The app will appear to cancel normally, but will silently send a DURESS alert with your location to your contacts and HQ.
💡 Remember: Your Duress PIN is your secret safety net. Never tell anyone what it is — not even someone pretending to be from your organisation.

Covert Alert — Shake to Trigger

If you cannot safely reach the SOS button — for example, because someone is watching you — you can trigger a silent duress alert by shaking your phone:

  1. During any active visit, shake your phone vigorously for about one second. The screen will flash red and vibrate.
  2. A confirmation screen appears: "Shake again to send covert alert." You have 5 seconds to confirm.
  3. Shake again to confirm. A silent DURESS alert is sent to your contacts and HQ monitor — identical to entering your Duress PIN.
  4. If you do not shake again within 5 seconds, the screen dismisses automatically and no alert is sent. This prevents accidental triggers from running or rough terrain.
